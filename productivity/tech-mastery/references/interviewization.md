# Interviewization

This stage turns mastery into answerable output.

## Required outputs

For every topic, produce all of these:

### 1. One-sentence answer

One sentence that identifies the topic and its purpose.

### 2. Standard interview answer

A short answer suitable for a normal technical interview question.  
Usually 1 to 3 paragraphs.

### 3. Follow-up questions

List likely follow-ups, such as:

- why was it designed this way
- what changed across versions
- what is the difference from a similar concept
- what common mistakes happen in practice

### 4. Project-style expression

Show how to answer:

- if the topic was directly used in a project
- if the topic was only encountered through a framework

### 5. No-direct-experience fallback

If the reader has no first-hand project use, provide a non-fake answer shape:

- where the topic appears in frameworks
- what they verified or read in source
- what design or review decisions they can still discuss honestly

## Quality bar

Good interviewized output should:

- sound natural
- preserve technical accuracy
- not collapse into buzzwords
- withstand at least one round of follow-up questions

## Anti-patterns

Do not produce:

- memorized slogans
- fake project stories
- long theory with no answer shape
- shallow “definition only” replies
